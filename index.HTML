02 — Process

How I Work

I already have experience designing and building modern web applications, especially on the frontend side, but SEG3125, Analysis and Design of User Interfaces at uOttawa has helped me better understand the theory behind strong design decisions.

Through the course, I've been learning more about visual communication, usability, interaction design, and user-centred thinking using resources like the Nielsen Norman Group's visual design principles. It's helped me think beyond just making interfaces functional, and focus more on making them intuitive, clear, and enjoyable to use.

My workflow is usually iterative: I start by understanding the problem and who the product is for, move into wireframes or architecture sketches, build incrementally, and refine based on feedback and usability considerations.

01

Understand

Define the problem and who it's for. No design work starts until the goal is unambiguous.

02

Sketch

Low-fidelity wireframes or architecture diagrams. Speed over polish at this stage.

03

Build

Implement incrementally. Keep it reviewable with small PRs, clear commits, real feedback loops.

04

Refine

Evaluate against the original goal. Fix what's off, cut what's noise, ship what works.
